## 2. Trading methodology — depth nuggets for content

*(Each of these is a proven Sean-ism the generator can expand into posts/threads.
They are specific, contrarian-friendly, and verifiably his — use them instead of
generic trading platitudes.)*

**Market context / correlations**
- Gold never moves alone: check **US30/US100** (risk-on/off), **DXY** (inverse),
  **US10Y** (opportunity cost), **VIX** (fear gauge) — as *confirmation tools,
  never entry signals*. "I'm not waiting for all six to line up — I just like to
  be aware of the wider market."
- Niche tell: **PAXGUSD** (gold-backed crypto token) trades 24/7 — a big weekend
  move on war/geopolitical news = anticipate a Monday **gap up** on gold. Small
  weekend moves are noise.
- Gold responds hard to geopolitics (missiles, war threats, tariffs) — he scans
  news over the weekend before planning the week.

**Structure & levels**
- His S/R definition is mechanical: **resistance = bullish candle followed by
  bearish candle; support = bearish then bullish — open/close only, wicks ignored.**
- **Never trade inside a range** — mid-range entries are a coin flip ("you are
  essentially gambling"). Only breakouts of the range high/low, or fakeouts from them.
- **Clean range** concept: few wicks + FVGs to the left = less friction = fast
  moves to the next level once a breakout occurs.
- Range must be worth trading: a $3 range on gold isn't; ~$8+ with clean air above is.
- If two resistances sit too close together, invalidate the nearer one.
- Late entries kill edge: if price broke out and already ran $15, you missed the
  explosive move and retracement risk makes it 50/50 again. **Proximity to the
  zone is the edge.**

**Sessions, volume, zones**
- Reads sessions through **4H candles** (UK time): Asia 22:00–06:00 quiet drift;
  06:00–10:00 pre-London/London volume enters; 10:00–14:00 pre-NY, ramping into
  NY open at 13:00 (fakeouts, sweeps, aggression); 14:00–22:00 NY + NYSE open at
  14:30 = huge volume, tapering to close.
- A level formed at 3am means far less than one formed at 11am peak London or the
  first hour of NY. **Zones need volume context to matter.**
- Don't take breakout trades in Asia or at the tail end of a session.

**Candlesticks**
- Wicks = liquidity ("where price hunted"); body = the real battle.
- Bullish candle with **no upper wick** = no range left to fill = continuation is
  ~50/50 — avoid. No *opposite* wick = a **volume candle** (dominance) — expect a
  pullback as the market rebalances.
- **Wick fills**: a HTF wick is LTF structure; wait for support to form, then a
  momentum shift back in the wick's direction before targeting the wick's extreme.
- "Run a mile" setup: push above a range high straight into a big engulfing
  rejection = classic trap.

**Execution discipline**
- **M30 only** for execution. Never M5/M1 — "I'm not interested in whipsawing
  price action inside a M30 candle." One timeframe, few zones, no chart-flicking.
- Checks correlated assets once or twice a week — not glued to six screens.
